Organization Design & Execution

Organization Design and Execution is about making sure the structure matches the work. The focus is on three things: making clear who owns which decisions and which work, removing the bottlenecks that slow capable teams down, and getting the operation ready for the workforce and technology changes already underway.

Most clients start with a conversation about what they already know is not working and what they want to be different. From there, the work becomes a project. We move at your pace. What we design, your team owns and runs. Most organizations at this scale see measurable value inside ninety days, in fewer escalations, faster decision cycles on critical work, and labor efficiency the CFO can see on the P&L.

Nobody's clear on who makes the call or owns the work.
By the time we sort out who decides, we've lost days and added rework. Things get escalated that should have been handled at the team level. It's not a people problem. It's that we've never had the time to sort out who actually owns what.
How we work through it
  • Find where decisions stall and figure out why it keeps happening
  • Build a simple ownership map your teams can actually use day to day
What changes for you
  • Decisions get made where the work happens, without unnecessary escalation
  • It keeps working when people leave or move, not just because the same people are still in place
Service: Accountability Architecture & Governance
Our people are capable. The structure keeps slowing them down.
The bottlenecks are not a skills problem. Sign-off chains longer than the work needs. Processes built for an earlier version of the business. Good people spending their time working around the system instead of doing the actual work.
How we work through it
  • Find where things consistently break down, with a view of your structure that's hard to get when you're running it every day
  • Change how the work actually flows so your teams can move at the pace they're capable of
What changes for you
  • Work that's been stuck for months starts moving
  • Your teams doing what they're actually capable of, not working around the system
Service: Execution Architecture
The technology is already here. We're still catching up to it.
New tools are running. Processes have changed. But nobody's defined who owns the calls and exceptions only a person can make, or what happens when something goes wrong. These weren't sudden changes. We just haven't had a chance to get ahead of them.
How we work through it
  • Map which roles have already changed and what's now unowned
  • Define who's responsible for what the technology can't handle, before a failure makes it urgent
What changes for you
  • Your team knows what they own in the new operation before something goes wrong
  • You stop reacting to changes that were already underway and start getting ahead of them
