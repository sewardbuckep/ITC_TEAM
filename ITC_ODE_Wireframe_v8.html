BLOCK 4, BODY COPY

We do not produce org charts. We do not deliver redesign reports. We do not hand your leadership team a new structure and a handshake. We redesign the work alongside your internal team, and leave your organization running it.

You have probably felt it for a while. Decisions that should happen at the team level keep landing on your desk. Roles were designed for an earlier version of the business. People carry accountability that exists nowhere in writing. You have sketched the redesign more than once and never had the runway to finish it. What you are short on is the time and depth to change it while keeping everything else running.

Most organizations do not schedule their structural problems. They get forced into them. An AI rollout that suddenly requires someone to define who actually owns a process. A post-acquisition integration where two teams are doing the same work and nobody has reconciled it yet. A leader departure that exposes how many decisions lived in one person's memory instead of in any defined structure. These are signals that the structure was under pressure long before the event made it visible.

Over the last fifteen years, middle market businesses have become far more complex in how they create revenue, allocate capital, and run their operations. Sales per employee and capital per employee have both climbed sharply, and supply chains, technology stacks, and decision paths have multiplied. The structures most organizations are still running were not built for that level of complexity. Accountability drifts informal. Workforce capability lives in people's heads rather than in any documented map. None of that feels like a crisis. Until something forces the issue.

Source: RSM US LLP, middle market revenue, capital, and complexity research (2009-2024).

That is where we work.

Most clients start with a conversation about what they already know is not working and what they want to be different. From there, the work becomes a project. We move at your pace. What we design, your team owns and runs.

Most organizations at this scale see measurable value inside ninety days, in fewer escalations, faster decision cycles on critical work, and labor efficiency the CFO can see on the P&L.

If that sounds like where you are, schedule a conversation.

BLOCK 7c, CAPABILITY DETAIL
Do any of these sound familiar?
Decisions keep stalling
Critical calls bounce upward and wait on the same one or two people. Everyone is busy. Nothing is moving.
How it works
  • Map where decisions consistently stall or get made by the wrong person
  • Surface roles where ownership is informal or assumed rather than assigned
  • Document clear decision ownership before the next change event makes it urgent
What you get
  • Decisions made at the right level without constant escalation
  • Accountability that holds when leaders or teams change
Service: Accountability Architecture
You're hiring for a role that no longer exists
The job description was written for a different operation. Onboarding discovers the mismatch 90 days after the offer.
How it works
  • Map what each role actually requires against what people can genuinely do
  • Identify where the gap between hire profile and role reality is concentrated
  • Give talent acquisition and onboarding a shared capability baseline before the next search opens
What you get
  • Hiring grounded in what the work actually requires, not what it used to
  • Development directed at the people and gaps that matter most right now
Service: Workforce Capability Mapping
The team is capable. The structure is slow.
Good people waiting on approvals. Meetings to schedule meetings. Decisions made twice because the first one didn't hold.
How it works
  • Identify which decisions are consistently escalated, delayed, or remade and trace the structural cause
  • Map the real decision paths, not the ones the org chart implies
  • Design decision flow around the speed the operation actually needs to move
What you get
  • Faster decision cycles on things that have been stuck for months
  • Teams that act with confidence because their authority is clearly defined
Service: Governance & Decision Velocity
Initiatives start strong and slow in the middle
Projects stall not because the team can't deliver, but because nobody knows who unblocks it when it gets stuck.
How it works
  • Identify where execution breaks down and the structural cause behind each point
  • Separate accountability gaps from process gaps from capability gaps
  • Redesign operating conditions so work flows without constant senior intervention
What you get
  • Visible improvement in execution speed within 90 days
  • Fewer initiatives abandoned mid-stream for structural reasons
Service: Execution Architecture
The transition is coming. The workforce isn't ready yet.
New technology, new roles, new requirements. An 18-month window and no clear picture of who on your team can actually make the shift.
How it works
  • Identify who in your current workforce has the foundation for what is coming
  • Define what the new roles require so development and hiring point at the same target
  • Build work arrangements that reach talent your current structure cannot access
What you get
  • A workforce ready when the new operation goes live, not months behind it
  • Development plans tied to the gaps the transition will actually expose
Service: Future-Ready Capability Building
The AI is in. Nobody knows who owns it.
Tools are deployed and processes changed. But nobody defined who owns the exceptions, the oversight, or what happens when the output is wrong.
How it works
  • Map which roles have changed because of AI tools already in use or being implemented
  • Define who owns the judgment, exceptions, and oversight AI cannot perform
  • Design the accountability structure for human-AI workflows before a failure forces the question
What you get
  • Clarity on who is responsible when an AI-assisted process needs review or reversal
  • AI adoption that holds because people understand their place in it
Service: AI-Driven Organization Design
